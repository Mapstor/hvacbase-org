## Safety Features to Look For

In 2026, portable generator safety technology has improved significantly. Look for these features.

**CO shutdown technology:** Generators with built-in carbon monoxide sensors automatically shut down if CO levels become dangerous. This feature is now required by UL 2201 standard and mandated in several states. Models with CO Guard (Generac), CO Secure (Champion), CO Minder (Yamaha), or CO-MINDER (Westinghouse) meet this requirement.

**GFCI outlets:** Ground Fault Circuit Interrupter outlets prevent electrocution. Most modern generators include GFCI-protected 120V outlets as standard.

**Overload protection:** Automatic circuit breakers prevent generator damage from excessive loads.

**Low-oil shutoff:** Automatically stops the engine when oil drops below safe levels, preventing catastrophic engine damage.

<Callout type="warning">
Carbon monoxide from portable generators kills approximately 70 Americans every year. NEVER run a portable generator indoors, in a garage (even with the door open), or within 20 feet of any window, door, or vent. See our complete CO safety guide for details.
</Callout>
