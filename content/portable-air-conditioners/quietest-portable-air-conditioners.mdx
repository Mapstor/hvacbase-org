## Understanding Portable AC Noise

Decibels are logarithmic — every 3 dB increase represents a doubling of sound intensity. Every 10 dB increase is perceived as roughly twice as loud by the human ear.

| Noise Level | Comparable Sound | Sleep Impact |
|------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 40–45 dB | Quiet library, light breeze | Excellent — barely audible |
| 46–49 dB | Refrigerator hum, light rain | Good — most people sleep through |
| 50–52 dB | Quiet office, moderate rainfall | Acceptable — light sleepers may notice |
| 53–55 dB | Normal conversation at 10 ft | Fair — noticeable but manageable |
| 56–60 dB | Dishwasher, busy office | Poor — disturbs most sleepers |
| 61+ dB | Washing machine, loud conversation | Bad — difficult to sleep through |

For comfortable sleeping, the WHO recommends continuous background noise below 45 dB. That's difficult for any portable AC, but units at 47–49 dB come close and are tolerable for most people, especially with background masking (fan, white noise).

<Callout type="info">
**Manufacturer noise ratings are measured at the lowest fan speed.** Expect 4–8 dB higher noise on medium and high settings. A unit rated at 49 dB may produce 53–57 dB on its highest setting. Always check specs at multiple speeds if available.
</Callout>

## Why Inverter Compressors Are Quieter

The two quietest units (LG and Midea Duo) both use inverter compressors. Here's why they're so much quieter.

A fixed-speed compressor has two states: fully on or fully off. Starting the compressor produces a noise spike of 5–8 dB above running noise. These on/off cycles happen every 10–20 minutes, creating jarring noise disruptions. At full speed, the compressor produces its maximum noise.

An inverter compressor runs continuously at variable speed. Once the room nears the target temperature, it slows to 20%–40% capacity. At reduced speed, the compressor produces dramatically less noise — often 6–10 dB below its full-speed rating. No start/stop cycling means no noise spikes.

The sound quality also differs. Fixed-speed units produce a rhythmic on-off pattern that's harder to sleep through. Inverter units produce a constant, steady hum that your brain adapts to and tunes out — similar to a fan or white noise machine.

## Tips for Reducing Portable AC Noise

**Place the unit on a vibration-dampening pad.** A rubber mat or thick carpet pad under the unit absorbs vibrations that transmit through the floor. This can reduce perceived noise by 2–3 dB.

**Keep the unit away from walls.** Sound reflects off walls, amplifying perceived noise. Position the unit at least 12 inches from walls when possible.

**Use sleep mode.** Most units reduce fan speed and increase the target temperature by 1°F per hour in sleep mode. This keeps the compressor running less aggressively and reduces noise.

**Maintain the filter.** A clogged filter forces the fan to work harder, increasing noise by 2–4 dB. Clean it every 2 weeks.

**Insulate the exhaust hose.** The hose carrying 110°F–140°F air produces radiant noise. Wrapping it in pipe insulation foam reduces both noise and heat radiation.
