# Smallest Portable AC Units (Compact Cooling Solutions)

The smallest full-featured portable AC in 2026 is the SereneLife SLPAC805W at just 39 lbs and 14.5 inches wide, delivering 6,000 DOE BTU of cooling for rooms up to 200 sq ft. If you need a compact cooling solution for a dorm room, small bedroom, RV, home office, or any tight space, these sub-50 lb units offer real air conditioning in the smallest possible footprint.

Compact doesn't mean weak — a properly sized small portable AC cools a small room just as effectively as a larger unit cools a bigger space. The key is matching BTU output to your room size.

## What Makes a Portable AC "Small"?

We define compact portable ACs by three metrics: weight under 55 lbs, width under 17 inches, and DOE BTU between 5,000–8,000. These units occupy 1.5–2.5 sq ft of floor space versus 2.5–4 sq ft for full-size models.

The tradeoff for compactness is cooling capacity. Small units handle 100–300 sq ft rooms — perfect for bedrooms, offices, and dorm rooms, but insufficient for living rooms or open-plan spaces.

## Compact vs. Full-Size: What You Give Up

| Factor | Compact (5,000–8,000 BTU) | Full-Size (10,000–14,000 BTU) |
|--------|---------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Room coverage | 100–300 sq ft | 300–550 sq ft |
| Weight | 39–55 lbs | 55–85 lbs |
| Floor space | 1.5–2.5 sq ft | 2.5–4 sq ft |
| Noise | 49–55 dB | 47–62 dB |
| Dehumidification | 30–65 pints/day | 70–111 pints/day |
| Features | Basic to moderate | Basic to premium |
| Price | $220–$390 | $350–$800 |

Compact units sacrifice cooling power and often features (fewer have Wi-Fi, inverter compressors, or dual-hose designs). But for appropriately sized rooms, they cool just as effectively.
