## What Makes a Good Bedroom Humidifier

### Noise Level: Under 35 dB for Sleep

The WHO recommends background noise under 40 dB for uninterrupted sleep. Most people sleep best with background noise under 30 dB. Look for humidifiers rated 26–32 dB — at these levels, the sound blends into ambient room noise.

### Tank Size: 8+ Hours Overnight

Nothing ruins sleep like a humidifier running dry at 3 AM and shutting off (or worse, beeping). For a typical bedroom, a tank of 1.5–2.5 liters provides 10–25 hours of runtime on low or auto settings. Larger bedrooms or very dry climates may need 4+ liter tanks.

### Humidistat with Auto-Shutoff

A built-in humidistat lets you set target humidity (typically 40–50% for bedrooms) and the unit maintains it automatically. Without this feature, you risk over-humidifying — which causes condensation on windows, damp bedding, and potential mold growth.

### Easy Cleaning

Bedroom humidifiers should be cleaned weekly to prevent mold and bacteria buildup in the tank. Look for wide tank openings, dishwasher-safe parts, or designs specifically engineered for easy cleaning (like the Canopy).

## Sleep-Friendly Features to Look For

### Auto Mode / Humidistat

Set your target humidity (40–50% for bedrooms) and the humidifier maintains it automatically, cycling on and off as needed. This prevents over-humidification and saves electricity.

### Sleep Mode

Some models have a dedicated sleep mode that dims or turns off display lights and reduces fan speed to minimum noise. This is especially valuable for light sleepers sensitive to glowing LEDs.

### Scheduled Shutoff

If your unit doesn't have a humidistat, a timer lets you run the humidifier for a set number of hours — enough for sleeping without running all day unnecessarily.

### Top-Fill Design

Middle-of-the-night refills are much easier with a top-fill tank. You don't need to flip the unit over or remove a tank — just pour water in from above.

### No Lights or Dimmable Display

Bright LEDs are a common sleep disruptor. Look for units with dimmable or completely disable-able displays. Some units remember your last setting and operate with no lights at all.

<Callout type="tip">
For the quietest possible operation, place your humidifier on a stable surface (not a wobbly nightstand) and away from walls. Vibration against surfaces and reflected sound can make a quiet humidifier seem louder than its dB rating suggests.
</Callout>
