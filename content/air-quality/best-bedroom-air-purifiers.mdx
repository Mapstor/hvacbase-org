## Bedroom-Specific Requirements

Bedroom purifiers need a different balance than living room units. Noise tolerance during sleep is 30–35 dB maximum for most people (equivalent to a whisper). But you still need enough CADR to hit 5+ ACH. The winning strategy: buy a unit with high max CADR and run it on a low or medium setting that's both quiet and effective.

## Bedroom Placement Best Practices

Where you place your purifier in the bedroom significantly affects performance:

**Optimal position:** 3–5 feet from your bed, at nightstand height, with the output aimed toward your breathing zone. This creates a "clean air corridor" around your pillow area.

**Never place:** Directly on the floor behind furniture, in a closet, or blocked by curtains. Restricted intake reduces effective CADR by 20–40%.

**Door closed:** Always. An open bedroom door connects your purifier to the hallway/house volume, dramatically reducing effective ACH. A 250 sq ft bedroom becomes a 1,500 sq ft house.

**Window closed:** During allergy season and smoke events, absolutely. Open windows introduce contaminated air faster than most purifiers can clean it.

<Callout type="tip">
**The oversizing strategy for bedrooms:** Buy a purifier rated for a room 50–100% larger than your bedroom. Run it on the lowest speed. You'll achieve your target ACH while staying under 25 dB. A Blueair 211i Max (rated for 420 sq ft) in a 200 sq ft bedroom runs on low at 23 dB while still delivering 5+ ACH. Whisper-quiet and highly effective.
</Callout>
