## What Makes an Air Purifier "Medical Grade"

There's no regulated definition of "medical grade" in consumer air purification. However, truly medical-grade units share these characteristics:

**H13 or H14 HEPA filtration** — Minimum 99.97% efficiency at MPPS. H14 (99.995%) for the most critical applications. Individually tested filters (not batch-tested).

**Sealed system design** — Gasket-sealed filter housings that prevent air bypass. Consumer units often have 5–15% air bypass; medical units target less than 0.1%.

**High ACH capability** — Enough CADR to achieve 12+ ACH in the target room, matching ASHRAE 170 healthcare ventilation standards.

**No ozone production** — Zero byproducts. No ionizers, no UV-C that produces ozone, no plasma generators.

**Independent certification** — Third-party tested and certified (not just manufacturer claims).
