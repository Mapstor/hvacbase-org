## Allergist-Recommended Practices

Based on guidelines from the ACAAI and AAAAI:

1. Use **True HEPA (H13) filters only**—not "HEPA-type" or "HEPA-style"
2. Size for **5–6 ACH minimum** in sleeping areas
3. Run **24/7**—intermittent use dramatically reduces effectiveness
4. Keep **doors and windows closed** in purified rooms
5. Combine with **allergen-proof mattress and pillow covers**
6. Use a **HEPA vacuum** weekly on floors and upholstery
7. Maintain **humidity below 50%** to discourage dust mites and mold
8. **Replace filters on schedule**—a loaded filter reduces both CADR and filtration quality
9. Avoid purifiers with **ozone-producing** ionization (can worsen asthma)
10. Place purifiers in the **breathing zone**—bedroom nightstand height, not hidden in a corner
