# Best Air Scrubbers for Construction & Mold in 2026

The B-Air RA-650 HEPA air scrubber is the best overall for construction and mold remediation, delivering 650 CFM with True HEPA filtration, negative air capability, and stackable design for under $700. For heavy-duty mold work, the BlueDri BD-AS-550 provides HEPA-sealed filtration with GFCI protection at 550 CFM.

For construction dust, renovation debris, and mold remediation, consumer purifiers are insufficient—you need commercial-grade air scrubbing.

## Negative Air vs. Air Scrubbing Mode

**Air scrubbing mode:** The unit recirculates and filters air within the room. Contaminated air enters, passes through HEPA, and clean air exits back into the same space. Use this for general dust control during renovation.

**Negative air mode:** The exhaust is ducted through flexible ducting to the outdoors (or an adjacent clean zone). This creates negative pressure inside the work area—air flows in through gaps and cracks rather than out, containing contaminants. Required for mold remediation (IICRC S520) and asbestos abatement (EPA regulations).

<Callout type="important">
**Mold remediation requires negative air.** Simply scrubbing air in the contaminated zone is insufficient—disturbed mold spores must be exhausted from the building, not just filtered and recirculated. The IICRC S520 standard mandates negative pressure containment for all Category 2 and 3 mold remediation projects.
</Callout>

## Real-World Project Examples

### Example 1: Bathroom Mold Remediation (80 sq ft)

A bathroom with black mold behind the shower surround. Setup: BlueDri BD-AS-550 in negative air mode, ducted through the bathroom window. 6-mil poly containment barrier at the bathroom door. 550 CFM in 640 cu ft volume = 51.5 ACH—far exceeding the 8 ACH minimum. The bathroom is under strong negative pressure; zero spore migration to the rest of the home.

### Example 2: Whole-Room Renovation (400 sq ft)

A full bedroom renovation including drywall demolition, flooring removal, and new drywall installation. Two B-Air RA-650 units: one in negative air mode (ducted out the window) and one in scrubbing mode (recirculating within the room). Combined 1,300 CFM in 3,200 cu ft = 24.4 ACH. Dust containment is excellent.

### Example 3: Post-Fire Smoke Cleanup (1,200 sq ft)

A home with smoke damage across the main floor. Three B-Air RA-650 units in scrubbing mode, positioned throughout the space. Combined 1,950 CFM in 9,600 cu ft = 12.2 ACH. Run continuously for 72 hours with activated carbon pre-filters to address both particle and gas-phase smoke residue.
