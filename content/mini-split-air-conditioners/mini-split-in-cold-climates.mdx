
**Protect the line set.** Insulate all refrigerant lines with thick (3/4-inch minimum) closed-cell insulation. In extreme cold, poorly insulated suction lines lose significant heat, reducing system efficiency.

**Install a wind barrier.** If the outdoor unit faces prevailing winter winds, consider installing a wind barrier (fence panel, shrub row) 3–4 feet from the unit. Wind increases the effective coldness on the coil, reducing heating capacity.

**Ensure proper drainage.** Condensate from the defrost cycle must drain away from the outdoor unit. In freezing weather, condensate can form ice dams that damage the unit. Use a heated drain line or ensure the drain area slopes away from the unit and is kept clear.

**Use a pan heater.** Most cold-climate units include base pan heaters, but verify this feature before purchasing. The pan heater prevents ice accumulation in the drain pan during defrost cycles.
